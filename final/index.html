Your Questions Answered

If you can't find the answer you're looking for, please contact us.

What is mental health?
Mental health refers to a person's overall psychological well-being. It includes how a person thinks, feels, and behaves.
What are the causes of mental health issues?
Mental health issues can be caused by a variety of factors including genetics, life experiences, and environmental factors.
What are some risk factors for mental health issues?
Risk factors for mental health issues include a family history of mental illness, substance abuse, chronic stress, and trauma.
What are mental health questionnaires?
Mental health questionnaires are tools that can help people assess their mental health. They typically involve answering a series of questions about thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.
What are mood and symptom trackers?
Mood and symptom trackers are tools that allow people to track their moods and symptoms over time. This can help identify patterns and potential triggers for mental health issues.
What is medication-assisted treatment?
Medication-assisted treatment (MAT) is a type of treatment for substance use disorders that involves the use of medications in combination with counseling and behavioral therapies.
What are complementary and alternative therapies?
Complementary and alternative therapies are non-traditional forms of treatment that may be used in conjunction with or instead of traditional therapies. Examples include acupuncture, meditation, and herbal supplements.
How do I find a mental health professional?
You can find a mental health professional by contacting your health insurance provider, searching online directories, or asking for recommendations from your primary care physician.
