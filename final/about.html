About us

ABOUT SALT HEALTH

Welcome to our mental health website, where we strive to educate and help people manage their mental health. Our mission is to promote mental health awareness, reduce stigma, and provide support to individuals who may be struggling with mental health issues. Our team is composed of mental health professionals, advocates, and individuals with lived experience in mental health. We understand that mental health is a complex and often stigmatized topic, which is why we are committed to providing evidence-based resources and tools that empower individuals to take charge of their mental health. Whether you're looking for information on common mental health disorders, self-help strategies, or professional support, we are here to help. We believe that everyone deserves access to quality mental health resources, and we are dedicated to making that a reality.

ABOUT OUR TEAM

Our team is composed of advocates, educators, and individuals with lived experience in mental health. Each member of our team brings a unique set of skills and perspectives to our work, but we are all united by our shared commitment to promoting mental health and reducing stigma. Our advocates have a deep understanding of the social and cultural factors that can impact mental health, and work to raise awareness and understanding of these issues. Our educators help to develop evidence-based resources and tools that empower individuals to take charge of their mental health. And our team members with lived experience offer a valuable perspective on the challenges of living with mental health conditions, and help us to ensure that our resources and tools are relevant and effective. Together, we are dedicated to providing accessible and effective mental health resources to anyone who needs them.

Contact Us

If you have any questions or concerns, please don't hesitate to contact us using the form above. Our team is dedicated to providing timely and accurate support to anyone who needs it. If you are in crisis, please seek help immediately by calling your local emergency services or contacting a crisis helpline.
