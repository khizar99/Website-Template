Self Care & Coping

Self-Care Tips

Take Time to Unwind

Sometimes we get so caught up in our daily routines that we forget to take a break and recharge. Make it a priority to schedule some time for yourself each day to do something that you enjoy. This could be anything from reading a book to taking a relaxing bath. Remember, it's okay to take a break and just breathe.

Practice Mindfulness

Being present in the moment and focusing on your thoughts and feelings can help you feel more calm and centered. Start by setting aside a few minutes each day to practice mindfulness meditation or deep breathing exercises. It may also be helpful to take a break from technology and focus on the present moment.

Connect with Others

Maintaining social connections with friends and family is an important part of self-care. Whether it's through a phone call, video chat, or meeting up in person, taking the time to connect with others can help reduce stress and improve overall well-being.

Coping Strategies

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • ADHD

Coping Strategies for Anxiety

  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Visualization techniques
  • Mindfulness meditation
  • Exercise
  • Spending time in nature
  • Practicing good sleep hygiene
  • Limiting caffeine and alcohol intake

Coping Strategies for Depression

  • Regular exercise
  • Setting achievable goals
  • Engaging in activities that bring joy
  • Talking to a trusted friend or family member
  • Seeking professional help (therapy or medication)
  • Practicing self-compassion and self-care
  • Challenging negative thoughts

Coping Strategies for ADHD

  • Break tasks down into smaller, manageable steps
  • Create a routine and stick to it
  • Use visual reminders and cues
  • Take regular breaks
  • Practice mindfulness and meditation
  • Use organizational tools, such as a planner or calendar
  • Seek professional help (therapy or medication)
  • Develop healthy habits around diet, exercise, and sleep
